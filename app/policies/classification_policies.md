# app/policies/classification_policies.md

## Classification Framework

This document provides guidelines for evaluating publisher websites. All classifications must be based on the following policies.

---

## Policy 1: Cashback Sites

### Definition
Websites that return a percentage of purchase value to users through affiliate programs.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Primary business model involves sharing affiliate commissions
- Clear mechanism for earning and withdrawing cashback
- Listings of partner stores with specific cashback rates
- User accounts and tracking mechanisms

### Decision Guidelines
Classify as cashback if the primary monetization is affiliate commission sharing with users.

---

## Policy 2: Adult Content

### Definition
Content intended for audiences 18+ that is sexually explicit or promotes adult services.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Presence of explicit sexual material
- Age verification requirements
- Primary purpose is adult entertainment or services
- Escort or adult dating services

### Edge Cases
- Educational sexual health content (not adult)
- Mainstream dating apps (not adult)
- Art with nudity (context dependent)

---

## Policy 3: Gambling

### Definition
Sites offering real money wagering on games of chance or skill.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Real money betting or casino games
- Sports betting or odds
- Poker rooms with cash games
- Lottery or raffle tickets
- Crypto-based gambling

### Indicators
- Deposit bonuses and wagering requirements
- Responsible gambling messaging
- Gaming licenses displayed

---

## Policy 4: Agency/Introductory Sites

### Definition
Sites that primarily redirect users to other sites for affiliate commissions with minimal original content.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Thin or aggregated content
- Heavy outbound affiliate links
- Limited original research or analysis
- "Best X" or "Top 10" list formats

### Quality Assessment
- High: Original reviews, detailed analysis
- Medium: Good content but heavy affiliate links
- Low: Thin content, mostly outbound links

---

## Policy 5: Scam or Low Quality

### Definition
Sites with deceptive practices or extremely poor quality.

### Evaluation Criteria
- Deceptive claims (prize winning, false scarcity)
- Technical issues (pop-ups, redirects, auto-downloads)
- Content quality (poor grammar, nonsense, stolen content)
- Missing legitimacy signals (no contact, privacy policy)

### Decision Guidelines
Flag if multiple quality issues present or any serious deceptive practice.

---

## Policy 6: Quality Scoring

### Score Ranges
- 90-100: Excellent, highly trusted
- 70-89: Good, meets quality standards
- 50-69: Acceptable, minor concerns
- 30-49: Poor, significant concerns
- 0-29: Unacceptable, automatic reject

### Score Factors
- Content originality and depth
- Trust signals (contact, policies, SSL)
- User experience and ad quality
- Business model transparency
- Industry reputation

---

## Classification Principles

1. **Context Matters**: Evaluate based on the specific site, not just keywords
2. **Primary Purpose**: Consider the main purpose of the site
3. **User Value**: Sites should provide genuine value to users
4. **Transparency**: Clear about business model and relationships

Remember: These are guidelines, not rigid rules. Use judgment based on the specific case.
